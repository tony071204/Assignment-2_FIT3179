Top 10 Most Visited Countries
The chart shows the top 10 most visited countries by international tourists
The United States leads global tourism rankings significantly with 930 million visitors.
China follows in second place with 660 million.
Ireland and India share similar visitor numbers around 170M.
Australia, ranks 11th with 77M.
Despite being outside top 10, Australia maintains strong performance.
The gap between USA and Australia is approximately 12x.
Top shares of gdp from tourism
This chart compares the share of GDP derived from tourism across several countries
Macao leads by a significant margin, with 52.3% of its GDP derived from tourism.
Other small economies like Guam (22%) and Palau (20%) also show high dependency on tourism.
Countries such as Fiji, the Philippines, and Croatia see moderate contributions from tourism, ranging between 8.5% and 12.6%
In comparision, Australia has a much lower share, with only 3.1% of its GDP coming from tourism.
This highlights the varying economic reliance on tourism across countries, with smaller economies relying more on tourism revenue than larger, more diversified economies like Australia .
Wealth Creates Travelers: GDP vs International Tourism(log scale)
Observation: wealthier nations generate significantly more outbound travelers.

Luxembourg leads exceptionally at 4,000 departures per capita.
European nations dominate high-income cluster (>$50K GDP) with 1,500-2,000 departures.
Singapore, Norway, and Austria show strongest travel propensity.

Middle-income nations ($10-30K) vary widely: 100-700 departures per capita.
African countries cluster at lower GDP ranges with minimal outbound tourism.

Key insight: Doubling GDP typically triples outbound travel rates.
Total Employment in Tourism Industries per 1,000 People
The heatmap illustrates tourism employment density from 2018 to 2022, highlighting global trends in tourism industry jobs per 1,000 people.
Macao consistently leads with the highest concentration of tourism workers, shown by yellow cells, especially during non-pandemic years
Australia shows consistently low purple values throughout years implies tourism is less of a dominant industry in this country.
Countries like Monaco and Macao, being highly dependent on tourism, also show high values,
while Spain demonstrates stable, moderate tourism employment.
The chart notably captures the significant downturn in tourism employment during the COVID-19 pandemic, with darker shading in 2020-2021 across all countries.

Comparison of Inbound and Outbound Tourism Spending Across Top 10 Countries
The charts compare inbound and outbound tourism spending across top 10 countries and Australia.
United States leads inbound tourism with $210 billion, reflecting a strong tourism sector.
China has a significant imbalance, with much higher outbound spending at $270 billion annually compared to inbound at $64 billion annually, highlighting its role as a major exporter of tourism
Countries like Germany and UK show a more balanced spending pattern, with slight preference for outbound tourism
Australia on the other hand, has high inbound spending relative to its outbound ($45B vs $37B), indicating it is a net importer of tourism.
